Statistical Analysis of Waiting Time Differences

1. Overall Distribution of Waiting Times

The Mann-Whitney U test was used to compare the overall distribution of waiting times between the two years, without assuming normal distribution.

Statistic Value

Interpretation: The proportion of patients seen within 4 hours decreased significantly from 45.5% in 2024 to 19.0% in 2025 (p < 0.0001). Patients in 2024 were 3.56 times more likely to be seen within 4 hours than patients in 2025.

2.2. 6-Hour Standard (360 minutes)
Statistic Value

Interpretation: The proportion of patients seen within 6 hours decreased significantly from 61.2% in 2024 to 31.0% in 2025 (p < 0.0001). Patients in 2024 were 3.50 times more likely to be seen within 6 hours than patients in 2025.

2.3. Extended Waits (>24 hours/1440 minutes)
Statistic Value

Interpretation: The proportion of patients waiting more than 24 hours increased significantly from 4.4% in 2024 to 24.6% in 2025 (p < 0.0001). Patients in 2025 were 7.12 times more likely to experience extended waits over 24 hours compared to 2024.

Summary of Findings

All three statistical methods yield consistent results, providing strong evidence that:

  1. The overall distribution of waiting times in February 2025 was significantly higher than in February 2024.
  2. There were statistically significant decreases in the proportions of patients meeting both the 4-hour standard (26.5 percentage point decrease) and the 6-hour standard (30.2 percentage point decrease).
  3. There was a statistically significant increase in the proportion of patients experiencing extended waits of over 24 hours (20.2 percentage point increase).
  4. The magnitude of these changes is substantial, with the median waiting time increasing by approximately 8 hours (494 minutes).

These findings demonstrate a clear and statistically significant deterioration in bed waiting times between February 2024 and February 2025.
