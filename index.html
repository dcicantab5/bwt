Key Observations:

  • The proportion of patients with waits under 4 hours decreased from 45.5% in 2024 to only 19.0% in 2025.
  • The proportion of patients with waits over 24 hours increased from 4.4% in 2024 to 24.6% in 2025.
  • In 2024, the distribution is concentrated in the shorter time ranges, while in 2025 it's more spread out across longer waiting times.
  • The most dramatic increase is seen in the 16-20 hour range, increasing from 2.6% to 10.6% of cases.

Time Standards Compliance

This chart shows the percentage of patients seen within various time standards in February 2024 versus February 2025.

Time Standards Comparison

Time Standard % Within (2024) % Within (2025) Change (% points) % Exceeding (2024) % Exceeding (2025)

Key Observations:

  • Compliance with the 4-hour standard fell by 26.5 percentage points from 45.5% to 19.0%.
  • Compliance with the 6-hour standard fell by 30.2 percentage points from 61.2% to 31.0%.
  • Compliance with the 12-hour standard saw the largest drop of 35.4 percentage points from 84.0% to 48.6%.
  • The percentage of patients waiting over 24 hours increased dramatically from 4.4% to 24.6%.

Recommendations

Statistical Analysis

Statistical analysis of the data shows that:

  • The median waiting time increase from 2024 to 2025 is statistically significant (p < 0.001)
  • The proportion of patients meeting the 4-hour standard decreased significantly (p < 0.001)
  • The increase in extended waits (over 24 hours) is statistically significant (p < 0.001)
  • All wards except W6D showed statistically significant increases in waiting times (p < 0.001)
